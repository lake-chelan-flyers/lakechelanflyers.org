Spectators are welcome at both launch and landing! Please follow these
guidelines for the safety of all.

## Spectators at Chelan Falls Park and the Junkyard Landing Zone

- Please watch for approaching gliders. Look up!
- Stay clear of the landing path of approaching gliders.
- After landing the pilot will move to the edge of the field. Please wait until
  pilots have cleared the field to approach!

## Spectators on Chelan Butte

### Launch Access

- Drive up Chelan Butte Road from Lakeside to [47.8044,
  -120.0387](https://goo.gl/maps/8XGskchgVq3tQMaN9). Chelan Butte Road is
  unimproved and its condition varies. High clearance and AWD/4WD is
  recommended. Do not drive the road when it is wet, as it can be slippery and
  you will damage it.
- Launch can also be accessed via [a
  hike](https://www.wta.org/go-hiking/hikes/chelan-butte).
- Due to extreme fire danger that is common during flying season, the Washington
  Department of Natural Resources has the following requirements:
  - All vehicles driving up the Butte must carry a shovel and a minimum 10/BC
    fire extinguisher.
  - Drivers must not drive off the road or park over vegetation.
  - Fires and smoking are strictly prohibited on launch.
- Parked vehicles must display a Washington State Discover Pass (available
  online and at several local stores including Safeway).
- Parked vehicles should not obstruct the upper loop road. Park on the gravel or
  mowed areas behind the launch area.

### Spectating

- Ask before touching any paragliding or hang gliding gear, even if it looks
  like the pilot needs help.
- Remain clear of pilots who are setting up to launch. It's best to stay uphill.
- Be aware that pilots must concentrate while performing their final safety
  checks and preparing to launch.
- Chelan Butte is known for dust devils, which can be very powerful in the
  spring and summer. If any equipment is threatened by a dust devil, DO NOT
  attempt to subdue it as you may put yourself at risk.
